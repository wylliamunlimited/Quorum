# AUTH-102 — A forgotten login shouldn't haunt you

**Type:** User Story
**Status:** To Do
**Priority:** Medium

## The want
> As someone who sometimes logs in from a shared or public computer, I want my
> session to not stay open forever, so that if I forget to log out, I'm not
> exposed indefinitely.

Support has seen this anxiety from users at libraries, hotels, shared laptops.
A login that never ends feels convenient until it's the thing that burns you.

## What "done" looks like (user outcomes)
- A login session does **not** last forever — it ends on its own after about a
  day.
- When it ends, coming back simply means logging in again. That's an acceptable
  experience.
- We are **not** building a "keep me logged in" / remember-me feature this round
  — keep scope tight.
